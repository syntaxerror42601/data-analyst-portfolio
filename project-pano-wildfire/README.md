# Wildfire Site Intelligence Analysis
Supporting Pano AI's Camera Placement Strategy

Analysis of 1.88 million U.S. wildfire records to identify high-risk 
states, counties, and ignition patterns to support camera placement decisions.

## Dataset
USDA Forest Service via Kaggle | 1.88M wildfire records | 1992-2015
Analyzed: 2005-2015 | SQLite database

## Tools
SQLite · DBeaver · Excel

## Key Findings
- High wildfire risk falls into two categories: fire frequency (Texas - 122,613) and average fire size (Montana 203.59 acres). High frequency does not always mean high-risk.
- Lightning causes 13% of fires making it the highest-risk ignition source. Because strikes are unpredictable, they are hard to detect through traditional observation methods.
- Rural counties, like Harney Oregon, have the fewest fires but 1 million acres burned. Weather, climate, and geography influence fire growth more than ignition frequency.
